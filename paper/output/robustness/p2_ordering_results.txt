P2: CHOLESKY ORDERING ROBUSTNESS
================================================================================

QUESTION
  Does the identified GDP response to a 100bp rate shock depend on the
  Cholesky ordering of variables in the VAR?

SETUP
  VAR(1) with trend='c', quarterly data 2004Q2–2025Q3
  Variables: tot (terms of trade), gdp, cpi, fx, rate (policy rate)
  Bootstrap replications: 2000 (residual bootstrap)
  Confidence interval: 5th–95th percentile of bootstrap distribution

  GIRF (invariant)         baseline vars (ordering-free)        -0.9169    0  -3.5108  -0.1312

GIRF FORMULA
  GIRF_j(h) = [A1^h × Sigma × e_k]_j / Sigma[k,k]
  where A1 = VAR(1) companion matrix, Sigma = residual covariance,
  e_k = unit vector for rate variable (k=4 in baseline order).
  Shock normalized to 1pp (100bp). Ordering-invariant by construction.

INTERPRETATION
  Stability of peak GDP response across orderings indicates the result is
  not an artifact of identification assumptions. The GIRF provides a
  fully identification-free benchmark.
