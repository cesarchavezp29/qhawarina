\subsection{Check-by-Check Notes}
\subsubsection{Check 1: Lag Selection}
VAR model information criteria across lags 1--3.

\textbf{Verdict:} Supports VAR(1) via BIC


\subsubsection{Check 2: VAR(2) Specification}
GDP IRF with two lags; tests sensitivity to lag length.

\textbf{Verdict:} Consistent


\subsubsection{Check 3: Rate Level vs.\ First-Difference}
Replaces $\Delta r$ with rate level; ADF confirms persistence.

\textbf{Verdict:} Qualitatively similar


\subsubsection{Check 4: FWL vs.\ Contemporaneous Dummy}
Algebraic equivalence between FWL and dummy-augmented VAR.

\textbf{Verdict:} Small differences


\subsubsection{Check 5: Alternative VAR Ordering}
Swap GDP and CPI in Cholesky ordering.

\textbf{Verdict:} Robust


\subsubsection{Check 6: Kilian Bias-Corrected Bootstrap}
Corrects small-sample upward bias in VAR coefficients.

\textbf{Verdict:} Robust to small-sample bias


\subsubsection{Check 7: Sign Restrictions with $h=0$ Added}
Adds GDP impact restriction; tests robustness of sign ID.

\textbf{Verdict:} Consistent with negative GDP effect


\subsubsection{Check 8: LP with Newey--West HAC SEs}
Local projections provide model-free alternative to VAR IRFs.

\textbf{Verdict:} Sign consistent


\subsubsection{Check 9: LP with Contemporaneous Controls}
Adds CPI, ToT, FX as contemporaneous regressors.

\textbf{Verdict:} Robust to macro controls


\subsubsection{Check 10: Proxy-SVAR Residual Correlation}
Diagnostic: residual correlation explains low first-stage F.

\textbf{Verdict:} High corr confirms d_rate proxies u_rate well


\subsubsection{Check 11: LLM Tone Construction Variants}
Three residualization schemes for LLM-based tone score.

\textbf{Verdict:} At least one construction has F>10


\subsubsection{Check 12: Dictionary Tone Instrument}
Dictionary-based tone as alternative instrument.

\textbf{Verdict:} Dict instrument weak F<10). F_B>10: dict tone corr with macro suggests exogeneity concern


\subsubsection{Check 13: Rate-Hold Subsample Tone}
Tone computed only from months with no rate change.

\textbf{Verdict:} Tone not predictive in hold months (expected)


\subsubsection{Check 14: Poverty with ToT Control}
Tests whether GDP--poverty link is confounded by terms of trade.

\textbf{Verdict:} Robust


\subsubsection{Check 15: Asymmetric Poverty Specification}
Separate coefficients for GDP expansions and contractions.

\textbf{Verdict:} No asymmetry (fail to reject H0)


\subsubsection{Check 16: Durbin--Watson / Prais--Winsten}
Serial correlation test and AR-GLS correction.

\textbf{Verdict:} No serial correlation


\subsubsection{Check 17: Breusch--Pagan Heteroskedasticity}
Heteroskedasticity test on poverty OLS residuals.

\textbf{Verdict:} Homoskedastic (BP p>10%)


\subsubsection{Check 18: Annual VAR Frequency Chain}
VAR at annual frequency for frequency-consistent poverty chain.

\textbf{Verdict:} Annual result diverges in sign


\subsubsection{Check 19: Joint Bootstrap Chained CI}
Joint uncertainty in VAR peak and poverty elasticity.

\textbf{Verdict:} Consistent with paper


\subsubsection{Check 20: FWL Residual Magnitudes}
Verifies FWL exactly zeros out 2020Q1--Q2 observations.

\textbf{Verdict:} Narrative restrictions on 2020Q1-Q2 after FWL are vacuous (constrain only ±within-dev)


\subsubsection{Check 21: Table 13 Framework Verification}
Country-level IT framework entries reviewed for accuracy.

\textbf{Verdict:} Brazil IT credibility and Mexico post-2020 stability are contestable entries
