## Q1: What if Qali Warma expands 20% (740,000 more children)?

**Current program:**
- Coverage: 3.7M children
- Budget: S/ 2,100M per year
- Benefit: S/ 567 per child per year

**After 20% expansion:**
- Coverage: 4.4M children (+740,000)
- Budget: S/ 2,520M per year
- **Fiscal cost: S/ 420M**

**Impacts:**
- **Child poverty: -0.13pp**
- National poverty: -0.16pp
- GDP: +0.17pp (local demand multiplier)
- Cost per person out of poverty: S/ 772,059

**Interpretation:** Modest poverty reduction, but high cost-effectiveness requires reaching poorest children.

**Current:** S/ 1,025/month

**Workers affected:** 2.5M formal workers

**Positive effects:**
- **Worker poverty: -0.30pp**
- National poverty: -0.38pp

## Q4: What if oil prices surge 30% but copper falls 10%?

### OIL SURGE (+30%):
Peru is a **small net oil importer**, so higher oil prices HURT:
- **GDP: -0.24pp** (import bill increases)
- **Inflation: +1.05pp** (transport costs → food prices)
- Export gain: +$480M (modest oil exports)
- Regional winners: Loreto, Ucayali, Piura (oil producers)

### COPPER FALL (-10%):
Peru is a **major copper exporter** (28% of exports), so lower prices HURT:
- **GDP: -0.20pp** (export revenues fall)
- **Inflation: -0.05pp** (deflationary pressure)
- Export loss: -$1,680M
- **Canon minero loss: S/ -1,122M** (fiscal hole for regions)
- Mining jobs lost: -3.6k
- Regional losers: Ancash, Moquegua, Arequipa, Tacna, Cusco

### COMBINED EFFECT:
- **Total GDP: -0.44pp** (both shocks negative)
- **Total inflation: +1.00pp** (oil dominates)
- **Interpretation: STAGFLATION RISK** — Growth slows while inflation rises. BCRP policy dilemma.

## Q6: What if Fed raises 50bp but BCRP holds steady?

### FED HIKE TRANSMISSION TO PERU:
- **PEN depreciation: +1.5%** (capital outflow, carry trade unwind)
- **Inflation: +0.38pp** (import prices rise)
- **GDP: -0.05pp** (capital outflow + uncertainty)
- Credit growth: -0.25pp (indirect via BCRP reaction)
- Transmission lag: 3 months

### IF BCRP HOLDS STEADY:
**Option 1: Don't follow Fed**
- ✗ PEN depreciates further
- ✗ Inflation pressures build
- ✗ May be forced to hike later anyway (behind the curve)

**Option 2: Follow Fed (raise 25-50bp)**
- ✓ Defends PEN from excessive depreciation
- ✓ Keeps inflation expectations anchored
- ✗ Slows domestic growth

**BCRP's dilemma:** Fed hikes create imported inflation. Peru must choose between currency defense (hike) or growth support (hold).

## Q7: What if BCRP raises 100bp to fight inflation?

**BCRP hikes from 5.75% to 6.75%**

### BENEFITS (Inflation control):
- **Inflation falls: -0.15pp**
- PEN appreciates: -1.0% (capital inflow)
- Anchors inflation expectations
- Credibility with markets

### COSTS (Growth sacrifice):
- **GDP slows: -0.20pp**
- **Credit growth falls: -1.2pp**
- Investment projects delayed (higher cost of capital)
- Consumer credit tightens
- Transmission lag: 6 months (slower than Fed)

### SACRIFICE RATIO:
**1.33** — For every 1pp inflation reduction, GDP falls 1.33pp

**RECOMMENDATION:**
- ✓ Worth it if inflation >4% (above BCRP target band)
- ✗ Too costly if inflation 2-4% (within target)

**Current context (Feb 2026):**
- Inflation = 2.3% (within target)
- No immediate need for aggressive tightening
- BCRP can wait for data

## Q8: Effects on SPECIFIC populations?

### SCENARIO: Expand all social programs 15-20%

| Group | Poverty Impact | Main Beneficiaries |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Children** | -0.13pp | Qali Warma expansion (+740k children) |
| **Elderly (65+)** | -0.14pp | Pension 65 benefit increase (+15%) |
| **Workers** | -0.30pp | Minimum wage hike (+15%) |
| **Rural** | -0.01pp | Indirect spillover from Juntos |
| **Urban** | -0.01pp | Indirect spillover |

### KEY INSIGHTS:
1. **Targeted programs work** — Each program benefits its target group most (children, elderly, workers)
2. **Spillover effects are small** — Helping children doesn't much help elderly, and vice versa
3. **Children and elderly see largest gains** — These groups have dedicated transfer programs
4. **Workers benefit from MW but face employment risk** — 562 jobs lost due to disemployment
5. **Geographic targeting matters** — Rural programs (Juntos) have limited urban spillover

### RURAL vs URBAN POVERTY:

**Rural poverty (currently ~40%):**
- Main program: Juntos (conditional cash transfer to 750k rural households)
- 10% Juntos expansion → -1.5pp rural poverty
- Limited reach of urban programs (minimum wage, Qali Warma)

**Urban poverty (currently ~18%):**
- Main programs: Qali Warma, minimum wage
- Benefits from formal sector wage increases
- Less affected by commodity shocks (except oil via transport costs)

**Policy implication:** Need DIFFERENT strategies for rural vs urban poverty. One size doesn't fit all.
